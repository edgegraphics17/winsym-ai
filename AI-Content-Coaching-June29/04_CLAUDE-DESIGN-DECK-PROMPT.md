You are my presentation designer. Build a **53-slide deck** titled **"AI Content Mastery"** for a 4-hour live coaching by **winsym.ai** (hosts: Karim Azzaoui + Tharisha Rishya). ## BRAND & DESIGN SYSTEM (apply to every slide)
- **Brand:** winsym.ai. Premium, sharp, consultant-grade. "I translate business into AI."
- **Colors:** Orange `#C45A1A` (primary accent), near-black ink `#1C1917` (backgrounds + text), cream `#F5F2EE` (light backgrounds), white `#FFFFFF`. Use a soft rose `#D295AC` ONLY when showing the GGC case-study brand, so the client's pink reads as "the client," not winsym.
- **Fonts:** Display/headlines = **Bebas Neue** (or a bold condensed sans). Body = **DM Sans**. Numbers/labels/captions = **DM Mono**.
- **Look:** Mostly dark (ink background, cream/orange text) for a premium keynote feel; switch to cream backgrounds for "proof point" stat slides so they pop. Big type, lots of negative space, one idea per slide. Thin orange rule lines as dividers. NO clip art, NO cheesy stock, NO gradient slop, NO bullet-heavy slides.
- **Layout grid:** consistent margins; section dividers are full-bleed ink with a huge orange numeral (PART 1–7). Stat slides use one giant number + one line of context + a small source citation in DM Mono at the bottom.
- **Footer (every content slide):** small "winsym.ai · AI Content Mastery" left, slide number right, in `#999`.
- **Accessibility:** body text never below ~24px equivalent; maintain strong contrast (cream/orange on ink, ink on cream). Never put small body text in rose on white.

## CONTENT RULES
- One core idea per slide; headline + minimal support. The speaker carries the detail — the slide reinforces, never competes.
- Every statistic slide shows the **number big** + a **source line** in small DM Mono (e.g. "Source: Wyzowl 2026"). Sources are listed per slide below — include them.
- Where a slide shows the GGC case study, use the rose accent and label it "LIVE CASE STUDY: GGC".
- Leave a **visual placeholder box** labeled "[LIVE DEMO — Claude/Higgsfield]" on slides where the speaker switches to a live demo, so the deck doesn't try to fake the demo.
