### "Everything that can go wrong, and the thing you forgot"

This is your insurance policy. Read it twice before the 29th. The single biggest risk in a live AI demo is not the content — it's **a render that stalls while 40 people watch.** Almost everything below is about removing that risk.

## 1. THE #1 RULE THAT SAVES THE WHOLE DAY

**Pre-generate every hero asset the night before and save it locally.**

Live AI generation is the wow factor, but it is also the thing most likely to fail (slow servers, queue, timeout, off-brand output). So:

- Run every key prompt the night before. Save the **good** outputs into a folder: `/Winsym Coaching/AI-Content-Coaching-June29/BACKUPS/`.
- During the session, you still generate **live** — that's the magic — but the moment anything stalls past ~20 seconds, you say *"While that cooks, here's one I made earlier"* and drop the backup. Nobody loses momentum.
- Record a **2–3 minute screen-capture** of the full "one prompt → brand" flow (Segment 4) as a last-resort fallback if MCP dies entirely.

> Pros don't gamble on live gen. They have a net and walk the tightrope anyway.

## 3. WHAT CAN GO WRONG → WHAT YOU DO (live failure playbook)

| Failure | Likelihood | Your move |
|---|---|---|
| **Render stalls / slow queue** | High | "While that cooks, here's one I made earlier" → drop BACKUP. Keep talking. Never watch a spinner in silence. |
| **MCP disconnects mid-session** | Medium | Reconnect calmly (you've practiced). If it won't, switch to the recorded fallback clip + show backups. Narrate it as "this is exactly why we set permissions properly — let me show you the recovery." Turn the bug into the lesson. |
| **Output comes back off-brand** | High | This is a GIFT, not a fail. Use P4.3 / R3 to fix it live. "This is the real workflow — you direct, you correct. AI is a junior who needs feedback." |
| **Venue wifi is weak/blocked** | Medium | Use your **phone hotspot** as primary or instant backup. Test it the morning of. AI tools die on bad wifi — don't trust venue guest wifi. |
| **Permissions stuck on "Always Allow" / nothing happens** | Medium | This is your planted teaching moment in Segment 2 — show the fix. If it happens unexpectedly later, same fix. |
| **A model/skill was renamed or moved** | Medium | Say what's on screen, not from memory. "Tools move fast — the principle is the same." Never argue with the screen. |
| **You lose your place / blank out** | Medium | This file. Glance at the 2nd screen, breathe, "Let me show you the next piece." Silence is fine; panic isn't. |
| **Credits run out** | Low (if you topped up) | Switch to backups for the rest; offer "I'll send the live-generated versions to everyone after." |
| **Someone asks a question you can't answer** | Certain | "Great one — I'll get you the exact answer in the Q&A / after, so I don't guess on stage." Park it. Don't bluff numbers. |
| **Audio/video clip won't play in the room** | Medium | Have MP4s downloaded locally, not streamed. Test playback in the room. |
| **Tharisha's transitions don't line up** | Medium | Pre-agree hand-off lines (Section 6). A 30-second "over to you" cue avoids dead air. |

## 4. THINGS PEOPLE FORGET (the full picture)

**Logistics**
- Confirm exact **start time and timezone** with every attendee + Tharisha (you have international audience habits — KL time, state it clearly).
- Confirm **platform**: in-person, Zoom, or hybrid? If online: who admits people, who watches chat, screen-share tested, recording on.
- A **co-host on the chat/Q&A** so you're not reading chat while demoing. (Tharisha owns this — confirm it.)
- **Joining link + calendar invite + reminder** sent 24h and 1h before.
- A **backup presenter device** (second laptop or iPad) logged in, in case the main one dies.

**Audience experience**
- A **one-line "what you'll walk away with"** said in the first 2 minutes (sets expectation, reduces drop-off).
- **Housekeeping up front:** breaks, where to ask questions, will there be a recording/resources, when they get the slides + prompts.
- A **resource pack** promised at the start and delivered at the end (your prompts library, the brand-brief template, the cost sheet) — this is also your lead magnet (Section 7).
- **Energy management:** 4 hours is long. The 11:50 break is your only scheduled one — watch the room; if energy dips around 13:00, do a 60-sec stretch/standup. Consider a second micro-break.
- **Names:** how to pronounce Tharisha and any VIP attendees.

**Content/brand**
- Decide **how much of GGC's real data you show on screen.** Sabina is a real person/client — show brand + product, but avoid exposing private contacts, revenue, or anything she wouldn't want public. Use her public story (it's already her brand) but keep internal ops off-screen.
- Have a **one-line disclaimer**: "GGC is a real brand we're using as a live case — everything you see is being built for them in real time."
- **Consistency thread:** make sure the brand brief (P0.1) is pasted BEFORE Segment 3 so every model demo is already on-brand.

**Commercial (this is a coaching that should sell)**
- Know your **call-to-action** for the end cold (The AI Academy scholarship — Tharisha closes, but you set it up all day).
- Have your **own** soft CTA ready (winsym discovery call / WhatsApp) in case attendees ask "can you do this for my business?" — that's a Company Brain lead. Don't let it walk.
- A **feedback form / next-step link** ready to drop at the very end.

**You, the presenter**
- Eat before. 4 hours on stage on an empty stomach is rough.
- Outfit on-brand and comfortable; test how you look on the shared camera if online.
- Practice the **opening 90 seconds** until it's automatic — that's where nerves hit hardest and where you win or lose the room.
- A glass of water and a clock you can see without turning around.

## 5. PACING & TIME DISCIPLINE

- You have **4 hours, ~9 segments.** The technical segments (Segment 4 one-prompt brand, Segment 6 Seedance, Segment 7 Content Factory) will **always run long.** Protect them by being ruthless on Segments 1 and 3.
- **Buffer:** treat every listed end-time as a hard stop. Keep one "cut line" per segment — content you'll skip if you're behind (marked in the run-of-show).
- If a live gen is slow, **talk through the concept** rather than waiting — the explanation IS the value; the asset is the proof.
- **Build in 60 seconds of silence-tolerance:** it's OK to let a render finish while you sip water and recap. Beginners rush; pros pause.

## 7. TURN THE COACHING INTO REVENUE (winsym layer)

This coaching is a stage. Stages create Company Brain leads. Don't let it end at "thanks everyone."

1. **Lead capture:** the resource pack (prompts library + brand-brief template) is delivered via a link that captures email → your owned list. Even a simple form.
2. **Soft CTA seeded all day:** whenever something lands, plant *"this is exactly what we build for clients inside a Company Brain."* Don't pitch hard — let the work pitch.
3. **The "done-for-you" bridge:** some attendees won't want to DIY. That's your highest-value segment. Have one line ready: *"If you'd rather have this built FOR your business, that's what winsym does — grab me after."*
4. **Follow-up sequence within 24h:** thank-you + resources + one CTA to a free discovery call. (I can draft this for you — just ask.)
5. **Content from the day:** record everything. One 4-hour session = weeks of LinkedIn/GMB clips proving authority → more leads.

> **Jarvis revenue filter:** This coaching's real ROI isn't the ticket price — it's the **Company Brain pipeline** it creates. Every attendee who says "can you just do this for me" is worth up to MYR 12,000. Plan to catch them.
